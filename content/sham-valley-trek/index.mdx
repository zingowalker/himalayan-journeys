## Description
> The Sham Valley Trek in Ladakh is a short and picturesque trek offering a glimpse of the region's stunning landscapes and culture. It's often referred to as the "Baby Trek" due to its relatively easy terrain and low altitude. The trail takes you through charming villages, lush fields, and ancient monasteries, providing a delightful introduction to Ladakh's natural beauty and heritage. Perfect for trekkers seeking a relatively gentle but rewarding Himalayan experience.

## Detail Itinerary

#### Day 1 - Likir - Yangthang:
Today you  will be driving from Leh to Likir, the distance by road from Leh to Likir is approximately 52 kilometers.

Likir is renowned for its  Monastery, a significant spiritual centre in Ladakh. After exploring the monastery your trek starts. 
The trail from Likir to Yangthang is a visual treat, offering amazing views of the Ladakh landscape.

You'll traverse through remote villages, where the serenity of the surroundings enhances the trekking experience. It's a captivating blend of nature and culture, making this day a true highlight of your trek.
> Distance & Time taken: 8Kms / 3-4Hrs trek / Elevation gain / loss: +431m / -274m

It’s a relatively short walk to Hemis Shukpachen village. We cross Tsermangchan La (3720m) to get to the village, which has its own beauty with a rich cultural background, myths and mysteries. There is a bunch of juniper trees on one side, which is not disturbed by the villagers for a superstitious belief they attach with it. There are a couple of guest houses and homestays in the village.
> Distance & Time taken: 10Kms / 3-4Hrs trek / Elevation gain / loss: +238m / -283m
